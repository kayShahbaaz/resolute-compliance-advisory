**Project Code:** RCA-P4

**Role:** GRC Auditor

## Engagement Overview

This engagement conducted a comprehensive compliance audit of a major healthcare provider in Jeddah, assessing conformance against the Saudi National Cybersecurity Authority's Essential Cybersecurity Controls (ECC-1:2018) and the Saudi Personal Data Protection Law (PDPL). The audit identified critical gaps in both cybersecurity controls and data protection compliance.

### Key Findings

- **NCA ECC-1:2018 Conformance:** 36.0% (41/114 controls implemented)
- **Saudi PDPL Compliance:** 2 of 10 obligations met (20%)
- **Critical Risk Identified:** Uncontrolled vendor remote access into live Hospital Information System (HIS) — Critical risk (L5×I5=25)
- **Privacy Concern:** No lawful basis established for health data processing under PDPL Article 23
- **Risk Profile:** Comprehensive risk assessment across cybersecurity and privacy domains

## Regulatory Framework

### NCA ECC-1:2018

Saudi National Cybersecurity Authority Essential Controls covering:
- Governance & Risk Management
- Access Control & Authentication
- Incident Management & Response
- Third-Party & Vendor Security
- Personnel Security & Awareness
- Critical Infrastructure Protection

### Saudi PDPL (Personal Data Protection Law)

Ten core obligations under PDPL:

1. **Lawful Basis** – Processing must have established lawful basis (consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, legitimate interests)
2. **Data Subject Rights** – Access, rectification, erasure, and portability rights
3. **Privacy by Design** – Data protection integrated into systems from design phase
4. **Data Minimization** – Collect only necessary data for specified purpose
5. **Purpose Limitation** – Use data only for specified, legitimate purposes
6. **Storage Limitation** – Retain data only as long as necessary
7. **Integrity & Confidentiality** – Implement security measures
8. **Accountability** – Document compliance and processing activities
9. **Data Transfer** – Restrictions on international data transfers
10. **Data Processor Agreements** – Contractual agreements with processors

---

## Critical Audit Findings

### Cybersecurity

1. **Vendor Remote Access** – Uncontrolled remote access from healthcare vendor into live HIS
   - No multi-factor authentication (MFA) for remote sessions
   - No session monitoring or activity logging
   - No termination date or scope limitations

2. **Access Control** – Excessive privileged access; shared service accounts

3. **Incident Response** – No documented procedures for healthcare data breaches

4. **Change Management** – Inadequate change control for clinical systems

### Data Protection (PDPL)

1. **No Lawful Basis** – Health data processing without established lawful basis under PDPL Article 23
2. **Consent Non-Compliance** – Existing consent mechanism does not meet PDPL requirements
3. **Data Subject Rights** – No mechanism to honor data subject access requests
4. **Processor Agreements** – Missing data processor agreements with healthcare service vendors

---

## Healthcare-Specific Considerations

- **Confidentiality Obligations** – Health data classification and protection requirements
- **Incident Notification** – Mandatory breach notification procedures
- **Patient Consent** – Special consent requirements for health data under PDPL
- **Vendor Security** – Heightened due diligence requirements for clinical system vendors
- **Business Continuity** – Critical infrastructure requirements for healthcare delivery

---

## Corrective Action Plan

**Phase 1 (Immediate - 4 weeks):**
- Audit and restrict all vendor remote access to HIS
- Implement MFA for critical system access
- Document lawful basis for health data processing

**Phase 2 (3 months):**
- Establish formal vendor management program
- Implement session monitoring and audit logging
- Update privacy notices to comply with PDPL

**Phase 3 (6 months):**
- Complete NCA ECC-1 control implementation roadmap
- Achieve PDPL compliance certification
- Implement data subject rights fulfillment process

**Phase 4 (12 months):**
- Achieve NCA ECC-1 compliance audit certification
- Maintain PDPL compliance posture
