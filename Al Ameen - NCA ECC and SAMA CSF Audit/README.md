# Al Ameen — NCA ECC and SAMA CSF Audit

**Project Code:** RCA-P3  
**Client Industry:** Insurance, Riyadh, Kingdom of Saudi Arabia  
**Engagement Type:** Compliance Audit  
**Duration:** July 2024 – October 2024  
**Role:** GRC Auditor  
**Frameworks:** NCA ECC-1:2018, SAMA CSF (Saudi Arabia Cybersecurity Framework)

---

## Engagement Overview

This engagement conducted a comprehensive compliance audit against the Saudi National Cybersecurity Authority (NCA) Essential Cybersecurity Controls (ECC-1:2018) and the Saudi Arabian Monetary Authority (SAMA) Cybersecurity Framework. The audit identified significant compliance gaps and critical infrastructure risks in a major insurance organization.

### Key Findings

- **NCA ECC-1:2018 Conformance:** 29.0% (33/114 controls implemented)
- **SAMA CSF Maturity:** 1.6/4.0 (Initial/Reactive stage)
- **Critical Risk Identified:** Shared Oracle Insurance production admin credentials — live Critical risk (L5×I5=25)
- **Risk Profile:** 28 risks across 8 domains
- **Governance Gap:** No formal cybersecurity governance structure or board oversight

## Regulatory Framework

### NCA ECC-1:2018

The Saudi National Cybersecurity Authority's Essential Cybersecurity Controls cover:
- Governance & Risk Management
- Technical Security Controls
- Incident Management & Response
- Third-Party & Supply Chain Security
- Personnel Security & Awareness

### SAMA Cybersecurity Framework

SAMA CSF maturity assessment across:
- Maturity Level 1: Initial (reactive)
- Maturity Level 2: Developing (managed)
- Maturity Level 3: Optimized (proactive)
- Maturity Level 4: Advanced (strategic)

---

## Audit Findings Summary

### Critical Control Gaps

1. **Shared Credentials** – Shared admin access to Oracle Insurance production environment
2. **Governance & Oversight** – No formal cybersecurity governance committee or board reporting
3. **Incident Response** – Undocumented incident response procedures
4. **Change Management** – No formal change control process for production systems
5. **Access Control** – Excessive privileged access; no just-in-time (JIT) provisioning

### SAMA CSF Assessment

- **Current State:** Maturity 1.6/4.0 (largely reactive, compliance-driven)
- **Target State:** Maturity 3.0/4.0 within 18 months (proactive, risk-driven)

---

## Corrective Action Plan

**Phase 1 (Immediate - 2 months):**
- Revoke shared credentials; implement individual admin accounts
- Establish cybersecurity governance structure
- Document incident response procedures

**Phase 2 (4 months):**
- Implement formal change management process
- Deploy privileged access management (PAM) solution
- Establish security monitoring and alerting

**Phase 3 (8 months):**
- Complete NCA ECC-1 control implementation
- Achieve SAMA CSF Maturity Level 2.5

**Phase 4 (18 months):**
- Achieve NCA ECC-1 compliance certification
- Target SAMA CSF Maturity Level 3.0
