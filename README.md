## About This Portfolio

This repository contains professional GRC (Governance, Risk, and Compliance) engagement deliverables produced through Resolute Compliance Advisory LLP. The portfolio spans five client engagements across two years, covering compliance frameworks applicable to the Indian and Saudi Arabian regulatory environments.

The portfolio demonstrates:

- End-to-end GRC engagement delivery — from engagement letters through fieldwork, findings reports, risk registers, and board-level executive summaries
- Technical depth across ISO 27001:2022, SOC 2, GDPR, India DPDP Act 2023, NCA ECC-1:2018, NCA ECC-2:2024, SAMA CSF, and Saudi PDPL
- Progression from implementation-side GRC Analyst work (Projects 1 & 2) to independent audit and assurance work (Projects 3, 4, and 3C)
- Practical application of Saudi Arabia's NCA cybersecurity regulatory framework across insurance and healthcare sectors

## Skills Demonstrated

**Audit and Assessment**
- Evidence-based control assessment and gap analysis
- Fieldwork checklist design and execution
- Stakeholder interviews and system walkthroughs
- CAP verification and remediation progress tracking
- Delta assessment methodology (framework transition)

**Risk Management**
- Risk identification, scoring (Likelihood × Impact, 1–25 scale), and treatment planning
- Risk register maintenance across multi-engagement client relationships
- Live risk identification and escalation

**Reporting**
- Board-level executive summaries
- Detailed audit findings reports with control references and evidence citations
- Corrective action plans with phased remediation timelines

**Frameworks (Technical)**
- ISO 27001:2022 Annex A (93 controls across 4 themes)
- SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria (CC1–CC9, A1.1–A1.3)
- NCA ECC-1:2018 and ECC-2:2024 (Saudi cybersecurity regulation)
- SAMA Cybersecurity Framework (maturity assessment)
- GDPR Article 28 DPAs, Article 35 DPIAs, data subject rights
- India DPDP Act 2023 (consent mechanism, Data Fiduciary obligations)
- Saudi PDPL (Article 23 sensitive data, SDAIA obligations)
