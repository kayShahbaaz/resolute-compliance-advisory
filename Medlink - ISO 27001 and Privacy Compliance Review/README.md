# Medlink — ISO 27001 and Privacy Compliance Review

**Project Code:** RCA-P2  
**Client Industry:** Healthtech, Mumbai, India  
**Engagement Type:** Gap Assessment + Privacy Compliance Review  
**Duration:** March 2024 – June 2024  
**Role:** GRC Analyst  
**Frameworks:** ISO 27001:2022, GDPR, India DPDP Act 2023

---

## Engagement Overview

This engagement provided a comprehensive ISO 27001:2022 gap assessment and privacy compliance review for a Mumbai-based healthtech organisation. The assessment identified significant security and privacy compliance gaps, including critical risks related to hardcoded credentials in production and non-compliant consent mechanisms.

### Key Findings

- **ISO 27001 Conformance:** 49.5% (46/93 controls implemented)
- **Critical Risk Identified:** Hardcoded AWS credentials in production codebase — live Critical risk
- **Privacy Compliance Status:** No GDPR Article 28 Data Processing Agreements (DPAs); Non-compliant DPDP Act consent mechanism
- **Risk Profile:** 31 risks across 11 domains
- **Processing Scope:** 8 data categories processed across 7 lawful basis categories

## Assessment Scope

- **ISO 27001:2022** – Full control assessment across all 4 domains
- **GDPR Compliance** – Article 28 DPA requirements, Data Subject Rights, Privacy by Design
- **India DPDP Act 2023** – Lawful basis for processing, consent mechanisms, data subject rights
- **Incident Management** – Data breach notification procedures
- **Vendor & Third-Party Management** – Data processor management and agreements
- **Access Control & Encryption** – Credential management, data protection at rest and in transit

---

## Critical Compliance Issues

### Security

1. **Hardcoded Credentials** – AWS credentials embedded in production codebase
2. **Credential Lifecycle** – No automated credential rotation mechanism
3. **Access Logging** – Insufficient logging of privileged access

### Privacy

1. **Missing DPAs** – No GDPR Article 28 Data Processing Agreements with processors
2. **Consent Non-Compliance** – Current consent mechanism does not meet DPDP Act requirements
3. **RoPA Gaps** – Incomplete Records of Processing Activities for GDPR compliance

---

## Remediation Priorities

**Phase 1 (Immediate - 3 months):**
- Revoke and rotate all hardcoded credentials
- Implement secrets management solution
- Establish Data Processing Agreements with third parties

**Phase 2 (6 months):**
- Implement GDPR-compliant consent mechanisms
- Update privacy notices to comply with DPDP Act
- Complete RoPA documentation

**Phase 3 (12 months):**
- Achieve ISO 27001:2022 certification
- Obtain GDPR adequacy assessment
